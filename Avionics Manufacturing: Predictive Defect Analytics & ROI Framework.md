# ✈️ Avionics Manufacturing: Predictive Defect Analytics & ROI Framework

## 📌 Strategic Overview
This project addresses the high-stakes environment of **Avionics PCBA (Printed Circuit Board Assembly)** production. By integrating Machine Learning with Industrial Engineering principles, I developed a framework to predict manufacturing defects and quantify the financial benefits of precision process control.

## ⚙️ The Industrial Problem
In avionics manufacturing, a single component defect can lead to catastrophic failure or costly rework. Traditional quality control is highly reactive; this project shifts the paradigm to **Proactive Quality Engineering**. By predicting defects before they occur, manufacturers can prevent scrap, reduce capital tie-up, and mitigate Aircraft on Ground (AOG) risks.

## 📊 Analytics Highlights
- **Predictive Engine:** Random Forest Regressor (Simulation-based Prototype).
- **Key Discovery:** Identified a critical "Failure Zone" driving defect spikes when **Reflow Oven Temperature exceeds 255°C** combined with **Ambient Humidity above 60%**.
- **Model Integrity:** Achieved high precision in identifying fault patterns, demonstrating the viability of real-time IoT defect alerting on the factory floor.

## 📈 Business Impact (The "RM 1.8M" Value)
Using data-driven environmental thresholds, the simulated ROI includes:
- **Yield Improvement:** Potential defect rate reduction from a historical baseline of **3.5% down to 0.5%**.
- **Cost Avoidance:** Estimated savings of **RM 1.8 Million/year** (based on standard PCBA production volumes and component costs in Northern Malaysia).
- **Process Optimization:** Provides a data-backed rationale for upgrading facility HVAC systems and standardizing Oven Calibration SOPs (aligned with Lean Six Sigma methodologies).
